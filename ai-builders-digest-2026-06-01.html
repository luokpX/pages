🏗️ Model Releases
Claude Opus 4.8: "A Modest but Tangible Improvement"
Simon Willison
May 28, 2026

Anthropic shipped Claude Opus 4.8 with a refreshingly honest description — calling it a modest incremental improvement. The standout feature: honesty. Opus 4.8 is ~4x less likely than its predecessor to let flaws in code pass unremarked, and had the lowest incorrect rate across every benchmark.

  • 4x reduction in hallucination/incorrect-rate compared to Opus 4.7
  • Mid-conversation system messages — append updated instructions mid-conversation without restating full system prompt
  • Lower prompt cache minimum: 1,024 tokens (down from 4,096 on 4.7)
  • Same pricing: $5/M input, $25/M output. Fast mode at 2x (down from prior 6x premium)
  • 1M token context window, 128K max output, knowledge cutoff January 2026

"One of the most prominent improvements in Opus 4.8 is its honesty. We train all our models to be honest — for instance, to avoid making claims that they can't support. But a general problem with AI models is that they sometimes jump to conclusions, confidently claiming to have made progress in their work despite the evidence being thin."

— Anthropic, Claude Opus 4.8 Announcement
