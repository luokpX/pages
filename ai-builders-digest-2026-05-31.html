📈 Three Mega-Trends Shaping AI Builders This Week

1. Knowledge Graphs Are the New Interface — For the second consecutive week, knowledge graph repos dominate the top of GitHub trending. Understand-Anything (+25,612) and CodeGraph (+15,909) collectively pulled 41.5K stars. The message is clear: AI agents need structured, graph-based code understanding to be effective. Builders are moving beyond "throw more context at it" to "give it a map."

2. The Anti-Slop Movement Accelerates — Taste-skill (+10,202), stop-slop (+3,543), and heretic (+1,248) form a growing ecosystem of tools fighting generic AI output. The community is tired of "I'd be happy to help you with that" prose and is actively building guardrails for quality. This isn't just a trend — it's the market maturing.

3. Agent Ecosystem Management Becomes a Thing — ECC (199K ⭐) continues its reign as the agent harness standard. This week's new entrants — Microsoft's agent-governance-toolkit, herdr (agent multiplexer), harness (meta-skill for agent teams), and claude-code-harness — signal the market shifting from building AI agents to managing fleets of them securely at scale.
