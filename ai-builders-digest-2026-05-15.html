Key Takeaways for Builders

  • 1.
    Ship receipts, not vibes. The best solo launches today turn a messy AI or subscription workflow into a report someone can forward to their team or finance department.
  • 2.
    Agent cost visibility is the wedge. 55,000-token connectors and $2,000 editor bills are creating a new category: "Who approved this and what will it cost?"
  • 3.
    Ownership is the meta-theme. From car telemetry to AI tooling to data sovereignty — the underlying question is always "Who controls this and who pays when it breaks?"
  • 4.
    Open-source needs a commercial control layer. Fast repos give teams power without governance. Sell policy checks, recurring drift reports, and team-safe defaults.
  • 5.
    Institutional penalties create product opportunities. arXiv's 1-year ban for hallucinations, Ontario's medical AI audits — compliance tools for AI accuracy have new buyers.
